Siok Falls and River Trekking Adventure

The adventure begins with a scenic journey to Sitio Siok in Barangay Mabini, roughly 11 kilometers from Koronadal City. Prepare for a moderate to challenging river trek, as the path involves navigating through

Rocky Terrain: Expect slippery rocks and uneven surfaces. Sturdy shoes with good grip are essential.
River Currents: The water level can vary depending on the season. Be prepared for wading through shallow waters or occasional deeper sections.
Breathtaking Scenery: Along the way, marvel at towering trees, lush vegetation, and the refreshing sound of flowing water.

Reaching the Reward: Siok Falls

After approximately two hours of trekking, your efforts will be rewarded with the sight of the majestic Siok Falls. This 40-foot wonder cascades down a rocky cliff, creating a captivating natural pool at its base. Take a refreshing dip in the cool water or simply soak in the breathtaking view.

The river trekking adventure site was formally launched last Tuesday October 2, 2012 attended by city officials and employees headed by Mayor Peter Miguel, the land owners in Sitio Siok (pronounced si-ohk), members of the media and some nature enthusiasts and adventure tourists. Take a look at some of the pictures as South Cotabato News joined the river trekking adventure and the launching of Siok Falls as an eco-tourism site in the City of Koronadal.

Planning Your Trip

Seasonality: The ideal time to visit is during the dry season (typically November to April) when the water levels are lower and trekking conditions are more favorable.
Guides: Hiring a local guide familiar with the terrain is highly recommended. They can ensure your safety, navigate the route, and provide insights into the local environment.
Gear: Wear comfortable clothing and shoes suitable for hiking and getting wet. Bring a waterproof bag for your belongings, a hat, sunscreen, and insect repellent. Don't forget a swimsuit and towel if you plan on swimming in the falls.

Responsible Adventure

Leave No Trace: Pack out all your trash and avoid littering to preserve the natural beauty of the area.
Respect the Environment: Minimize noise pollution and avoid disturbing the wildlife.
Be mindful of your safety: Always follow your guide's instructions and be aware of the surroundings.
