Tri- People Monument | Surallah Cultural Landmark

The Surallah Cultural Landmark also called the Tri-People Monument is the province's newest attraction. An artwork by acclaimed Mindanao sculpture Kublai Ponce Millan, the landmark was unveiled last March 15, 2011. It gives tribute to the tri-people living in the area - the T'bolis, Muslims and the Christian settlers. It also highlights Surallah's local produce.

At the center of this landmark is a towering 60-feet high 2-string indigenous T'boli musical instrument called Hegalong surrounded by huge elongated leaves of endemic bulb plant called Bohung. Around the Bohung are two giant drums called T'nonggong, gigantic sculpted human figures of T'bolis, Muslims and Christians playing musical instruments, dancing and smiling while others holding local produce - grains, vegetables, fish and fruits.

Surrounding the hegalong stand three distinct figures: pen_spark

The T'boli woman: Adorned in traditional T'boli attire, she represents the community's resilience, artistry, and deep connection to nature.

The Ubo Manobo warrior: Standing tall and resolute, he embodies the strength, bravery, and rich cultural traditions of the Ubo Manobo people.

The Maguindanao datu (chieftain): Dressed in regal attire, he represents the leadership, wisdom, and rich history of the Maguindanao community.

The Surallah Tri-People Monument serves as a gateway to understanding the diverse cultures that enrich Surallah. Visitors can gain insights into the traditional clothing, music, and customs of each community. Local artisans often set up stalls near the monument, showcasing their beautiful and intricate handicrafts, offering a chance to take home a piece of Surallah's cultural heritage. pen_spark

Planning Your Visit to Surralah Tri-People Monument

Getting There

The Surallah Tri-People Monument is located in Barangay Poblacion, Surallah, South Cotabato. If you're coming from Koronadal City, you can take a jeepney or van heading to Surallah. The travel time is approximately 45 minutes. The monument is just a few minutes away from the town center, so you can easily reach it by tricycle or habal-habal (motorcycle taxi).
