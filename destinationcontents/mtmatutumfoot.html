Foot of Mt. Matutum, a strawberry park beckons to tourists.

Nestled at the foothills of the majestic Mt. Matutum, South Cotabato’s landmark peak towering at 2,286 meters, the strawberry park is surrounded by greenery and has a cool climate, especially towards dawn, all-year round.

Located in Sitio Amgu-o, Barangay Landan, some 30 minutes away from the national highway, Moso Strawberry Agritourism Park is the first strawberry farm in Polomolok but second in South Cotabato.

The Moso Strawberry Park has a vantage view of Mt. Matutum and the rolling hills surrounding the mountain, declared by then President Fidel Ramos in 1995 as a protected landscape due to its diverse flora and fauna as well as rare wildlife species.

Besides the enchanting view of Mt. Matutum, the four-hectare Moso Strawberry Park’s main attraction is allowing visitors to experience strawberry picking in their property where fruit trees such as durian, banana, lanzones and rambutan are also grown.

It is best to wear thick clothes and bring your own jackets, and yes, tents for protection against the cold night. The park has no accommodation facilities for now so visitors can bring their own tents or rent from them.

For souvenir, the park sells strawberry seedlings at 60 pesos each, with Joan advising they should be grown in shaded areas in the lowlands. Visitors can also bring home the strawberries they picked, the amount depending on the number of grams. “We’re doing it one small step at a time. We started this venture and we hope to sustain it,” she said, expressing optimism the strawberry park can attract more visitors on better days. (Bong S. Sarmiento / MindaNews)

Planning Your Visit to Mt. Matutum

Getting There

The Polomok Mt. Matutum Strawberry Park is open to visitors daily. The entrance fee is minimal, making it an affordable and enjoyable destination for families and friends.

The best time to visit the strawberry park is during the harvest season, which typically runs from November to April. However, it's always best to call ahead to confirm availability before your visit. Wear comfortable clothing and shoes suitable for walking on uneven terrain. Bring sun protection, especially if you're visiting during the midday sun. If you plan on picking strawberries, bring a basket or container to collect your bounty. Cash is the most commonly accepted mode of payment.
